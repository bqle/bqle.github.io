During freshman Spring and Summer, I interned at Photon Commerce, a fintech company based in San Francisco, CA that aims to automate the parsing and payment of financial documents. There, I developed efficient algorithms for extracting key fields from invoices, clustering algorithms to identify key templates from a dataset of 100k+ images. To culminate my internship, I proposed and developed a REST API wrapper with over 60 endpoints and 2000 lines of code around the Federal Reserve’s new FedNow service that allows banks to clear and settle transfers near real-time. Technical Coursework

Theory of Computation
CIS 2620 focuses on the fundamentals of the theory of computation. The topics include finite automata and regular languages, context-free grammars and pushdown automata, Turing machines and undecidability, tractability and NP-completeness.
Data Structures and Algorithms
CIS 1210 is a fundamental course that focuses on the basic data structures and algorithms using the Java language. Topics include stacks, trees, maps, trees, and graphs and how to use them to solve difficult problems efficiently.
Programming Languages and Techniques
CIS 1200 uses OCaml and Java to introduce students to programming design, introducing topics like data types and representation, abstraction, test-driven development, programming patterns, functional programming, and object-oriented programming.
Computer Operating Systems
CIS 3800 is an introduction to the theory and practice behind modern computer operating systems. Topics include what an operating system does, system calls and interfaces, processes and threads, concurrent programming, resource scheduling and management, virtual memory, deadlocks, and virtualization.
Computer Graphics
CIS 5600 covers the technical aspects of 3D Modeling including viewing transformations, polygon and mesh data structures, keyframe animation, and interactive manipulation. We write a series program assignments in C++ that build up to a 3D game final project.
Real Analysis
A study of the foundations of the differential and integral calculus, including the real numbers and elementary topology, continuous and differentiable functions, and more.
Complex Analysis
A study of the complex numbers, DeMoivre's theorem, complex valued functions of a complex variable, the derivative, analytic functions, the Cauchy-Riemann equations, and more.
Ordinary & Partial Differential Equations
Ordinary & partial differential equations and their solutions including solutions of the wave, heat and Laplace equations, and Sturm-Liouville problems.
Modern Algebra
Theory of abstract algebra, with particular emphasis on groups, quotient groups, symmetry groups in three dimensions, Pólya-Burnside method, rings and fields, polynomial and Euclidean rings, quotient rings, field extensions, and geometrical constructions.
